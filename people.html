Research

Principal Investigator

Hamid Jafarnejad Sani, Ph.D.

Dr. Hamid Jafarnejad Sani is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Stevens Institute of Technology. Before joining the faculty at Stevens, he was a postdoctoral research associate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). Hamid received his Ph.D. degree in Mechanical Engineering from UIUC in 2018, where he worked in the Advanced Controls Research Laboratory. He got his B.S. and M.S. degrees both in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Tehran in 2011 and the University of Calgary in 2013, respectively. His research interests include control, optimization, robotics, machine learning, and cyber-physical security. In particular, he is interested in resilient control of intelligent autonomous systems in uncertain and adversarial environments.

Graduate Students

Mohamad Bahrami, Ph.D. Student
mbahrami [at] stevens [dot] edu

Mohamad received the B.S. and M.S. degrees in ME from Amirkabir University of Technology (AUT) in 2015 and 2017, respectively. He also served as a Research Assistant at AUT for three years and did research on performance problems of actuators and on nonlinear controller-observer algorithms. He is currently a Ph.D. student in ME at Stevens, where he is the recipient of the Provost Fellowship Award in 2019. His research interests include control theory and applications to dynamical systems.

Ziyad Abouelenin, Ph.D. Student
zabouele [at] stevens [dot] edu

Ziyad Abouelenin holds a bachelor of science in Mechatronics Engineering from the German University in Cairo. He did his senior project at NYU, where he designed and controlled an EMG-controlled under-actuated robotic hand. Currently, he is a PhD student at Stevens Institute of Technology. His research focuses on the development of a high fidelity simulation for the test and verification for multi-agent mobile robots.
